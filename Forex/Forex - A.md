 History 

  

Barter System – Direct goods exchange, no currency.

  

Gold Standard (1800s–1914) – Currencies backed by gold. Stability but rigid.

  

Bretton Woods (1944–1971) – USD tied to gold, all other currencies tied to USD. Dollar became reserve currency.

  

Nixon Shock (1971) – Gold standard abandoned, free-floating exchange rates start.

  

Modern Forex – Largest market ($7.5+ trillion daily), 24/5 trading, driven by banks, funds, and retail.

  

Impact today:

  

Floating exchange = high volatility.

  

Dollar still dominates global trade.

  

Tech evolution → algo trading, retail access.
